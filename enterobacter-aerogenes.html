Enterobacter aerogenes

A Gram-negative bacterium associated with hospital-acquired infections, especially in immunocompromised hosts

Overview

Enterobacter aerogenes is an opportunistic pathogen known for causing nosocomial infections such as urinary tract infections, bloodstream infections, and respiratory tract infections. Its multidrug resistance makes treatment challenging in healthcare settings.

Molecular Markers

  • Extended-Spectrum Beta-Lactamases (ESBL) production conferring resistance to beta-lactam antibiotics
  • Carbapenemase enzymes leading to carbapenem resistance
  • Efflux pump mechanisms reducing intracellular antibiotic concentration
  • Biofilm formation contributing to persistence on medical devices

Immune Response

Host immune response includes neutrophil-mediated clearance. Biofilm production and multidrug resistance mechanisms help the bacteria evade immune detection and destruction, complicating infections in immunocompromised patients.

Epidemiology

  • Frequently isolated in hospital settings, particularly ICUs
  • Common in patients with invasive devices such as catheters and ventilators
  • Higher infection rates in immunocompromised individuals
