Polyomavirus

A DNA virus causing latent infections, reactivating under immunosuppression and leading to hemorrhagic cystitis in transplant patients

Overview

Polyomaviruses are small, non-enveloped DNA viruses capable of establishing persistent infections in the urinary tract. Reactivation of latent infection can cause hemorrhagic cystitis, particularly in immunocompromised and transplant patients.

Molecular Markers

  • Detection of viral DNA by PCR
  • Large T antigen involved in viral replication and host interaction
  • VP1 capsid protein used for serological assays

Immune Response

Cell-mediated immunity plays a critical role in controlling Polyomavirus infection. In immunosuppressed individuals, virus reactivation may cause disease, including hemorrhagic cystitis.

Epidemiology

  • Common in renal and bone marrow transplant recipients
  • Associated with hemorrhagic cystitis and nephropathy
  • Reactivation linked to severe immunosuppression
