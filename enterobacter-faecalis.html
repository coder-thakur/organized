Enterococcus faecalis

A Gram-positive bacterium associated with persistent urinary tract infections and biofilm formation on catheters

Overview

Enterococcus faecalis is a Gram-positive cocci involved in healthcare-associated infections, notably urinary tract infections. Its ability to form biofilms and survive harsh environments contributes to its persistence in hospital settings.

Molecular Markers

  • Adhesins for attachment to host tissues and medical devices
  • Cytolysin contributing to virulence and host cell lysis
  • Vancomycin resistance determinants (e.g., VanA, VanB)
  • Biofilm-forming genes aiding in persistent colonization

Immune Response

E. faecalis can evade the host immune response by forming biofilms, making clearance difficult. Persistent infections are common, especially in patients with indwelling catheters or compromised immunity.

Epidemiology

  • Common nosocomial pathogen
  • Frequently isolated from catheter-associated urinary tract infections (CAUTIs)
  • Often involved in polymicrobial infections
