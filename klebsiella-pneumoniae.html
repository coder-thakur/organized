Klebsiella pneumoniae

A Gram-negative pathogen associated with multidrug-resistant urinary tract infections in healthcare settings

Overview

Klebsiella pneumoniae is a major cause of hospital-acquired infections including UTIs, pneumonia, and septicemia. Its polysaccharide capsule and multiple resistance genes make it a serious clinical concern, particularly in ICU and catheterized patients.

Molecular Markers

  • Capsular polysaccharide contributing to antiphagocytic activity
  • magA and rmpA genes associated with hypermucoviscosity phenotype
  • Siderophore systems (e.g., enterobactin, yersiniabactin) for iron acquisition
  • MLST (Multilocus Sequence Typing) used for strain differentiation

Immune Response

The capsule allows evasion of phagocytosis and complement-mediated killing. Infected individuals show a neutrophilic inflammatory response, but MDR strains are harder to clear due to extensive drug resistance.

Epidemiology

  • Common nosocomial pathogen in ICUs and catheterized patients
  • Multidrug resistance (MDR) complicates treatment options
  • Frequently isolated in urinary tract and bloodstream infections
