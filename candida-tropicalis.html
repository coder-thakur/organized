Candida tropicalis

A pathogenic yeast linked with UTIs, known for high biofilm production and drug resistance

Overview

Candida tropicalis is associated with catheter-associated urinary tract infections and bloodstream infections, especially in immunocompromised individuals.

Molecular Markers

  • Adhesins for surface attachment
  • Enzymes such as proteases and lipases
  • Azole resistance via efflux pumps
  • Biofilm formation on medical devices

Immune Response

Neutrophils play a key role in clearance; biofilm formation shields the pathogen from immune response, increasing persistence in immunocompromised hosts.

Epidemiology

  • Common in catheterized and diabetic patients
  • Significant hospital-acquired pathogen
