Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

A viral pathogen associated with UTIs in immunocompromised individuals

Overview

Cytomegalovirus (CMV) is a DNA virus of the Herpesviridae family. While asymptomatic in healthy individuals, CMV can cause significant disease in immunocompromised patients. In the urinary tract, CMV may lead to hemorrhagic cystitis or viral shedding in urine post-transplant or during congenital infection.

Molecular Markers

  • dsDNA genome ~235 kb with >200 genes
  • Immediate-early genes (IE1/IE2) for viral replication and latency
  • pp65 antigen used in diagnostic assays
  • UL97 gene mutations linked to antiviral resistance

Immune Response

CMV activates both innate and adaptive immunity. CD8+ T-cells and NK cells play key roles in viral clearance. In immunosuppressed patients, the virus evades immunity via downregulating MHC I and expressing viral cytokine homologs.

Epidemiology

  • CMV seroprevalence >60% globally
  • Associated with congenital infections and post-transplant complications
  • Urinary tract involvement noted in neonatal and transplant cases

Treatment

  • Antivirals: Ganciclovir (IV), Valganciclovir (oral)
  • Resistant strains: Foscarnet or Cidofovir
  • Preemptive therapy based on viral load monitoring post-transplant
  • CMV vaccines are under investigation but not yet approved
