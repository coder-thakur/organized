Candida lusitanae

A rare Candida species with inherent resistance to amphotericin B and increasing clinical relevance

Overview

Candida lusitanae is a non-albicans yeast species known for its unusual resistance to amphotericin B. It is emerging in nosocomial environments and can cause urinary tract infections, particularly in immunocompromised or catheterized patients.

Molecular Markers

  • Low ergosterol content affecting amphotericin B binding
  • Mutations in the ERG gene family (especially ERG3, ERG6)
  • Enzyme secretion: lipases and proteases involved in host tissue invasion
  • Biofilm formation contributes to catheter colonization and persistence

Immune Response

Immunity is mainly mediated by neutrophils and monocytes. Recognition occurs via pattern recognition receptors like TLR2 and dectin-1. In immunocompromised individuals, biofilms and immune modulation mechanisms may lead to persistent infection.

Epidemiology

  • Rare but increasingly reported in hospital settings
