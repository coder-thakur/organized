Proteus mirabilis

A Gram-negative bacterium commonly associated with complicated urinary tract infections and kidney stone formation

Overview

Proteus mirabilis is an important uropathogen frequently involved in catheter-associated and complicated UTIs. It exhibits strong motility and urease activity, leading to struvite stone formation.

Molecular Markers

  • Urease enzyme promoting stone formation by increasing urine pH
  • Flagella mediating swarming motility
  • Adhesins facilitating colonization of urinary tract surfaces
  • Hemolysin contributing to tissue damage

Immune Response

The host immune system mounts an inflammatory response against P. mirabilis, but biofilm formation on catheters provides a protective niche, allowing persistence and recurrent infections.

Epidemiology

  • Predominantly found in catheterized patients
  • Common cause of complicated UTIs and kidney stone formation
  • Forms biofilms on urinary catheters
